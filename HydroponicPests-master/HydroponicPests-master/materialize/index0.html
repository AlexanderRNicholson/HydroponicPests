Hydroponic Pests

Main content

When you build a hydroponic system of any size, you're going to have to deal with pests at some point. If you're looking for advice on how to protect your hydroponic garden and stop common pests like spider mites, fungus gnats and whiteflies, you're in the right place.

Common Pests

Aphids

Also known as greenflies or plant lice, these sap-sucking insects are loved by ants, less so by gardeners.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Aphids often congregate near stems at the tops of plants. Ants are drawn to aphids, so a gathering of ants is a strong indication.
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    Avoid cross-contamination: don't use soil or tools from your exterior garden in your hydroponic set-up. Aphids like synthetic fertilizers.
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Use insecticidal soap. Water washes aphids away.
Whiteflies
❮ ❯

Whiteflies can be persistent pests in hydroponic gardens.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Whiteflies feed on leaves' undersides. Their feeding causes yellowing of leaves and white spots.
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    Whiteflies multiply faster in warm conditions.
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Whiteflies will relocate to nearby plants if disturbed, so apply insecticide to all plants in the area of an infestation. Scraping the eggs off leaves' undersides and applying neem oil will significantly disrupt nymph growth.
Spider Mites
❮ ❯

These tiny spiders can be hard to detect, but are a danger to plants all the same.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Spider mites spin webbing that can often be easier to spot than the insects themselves. If you see webbing, try wiping the underside of a leaf with a tissue and check it for red streaks.
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    Spider mites enjoy low humidity and breed faster in high temperatures.
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Isolate affected plants. Pyrethrin stops them from feeding. Washing leaves in warm, soapy water is effective. Insecticides work, but they can breed resistance over time.
Thrips

Thrips are slender but hungry pests.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Thrips cause browning and drying out of leaves. Look for yellow spots and small dark metallic specks on leaves (thrip feces).
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    Thrips multiply and mature faster in warm conditions.
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Thrips can be removed by hand, but their eggs (small, white and sticky) should be wiped from leaves. Apply insecticidal soap to affected plants.

Michigan Pests

Scale

Scale insects get their name from the females of the type, which look like fish or reptile scales.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Scale bugs affix themselves to stems or the undersides of leaves, and will look like peculiar growths more than insects. They feed on sap.
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    Scale bugs are vulnerable to systemic pesticides that poison plants' sap, or predator bugs like parasitic wasps and coccinellid beetles..
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Their covering makes them resistant to contact insecticides. Remove them and treat affected plants with horticultural oils or insecticidal soap.
Mealybugs
❮ ❯

Mealybugs are a kind of unarmored scale bug that like the warm.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Mealybugs stick to leaf stem joints. They excrete a white grainy powder.
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    Cultivate ladybugs: they're voracious mealybug predators.
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Scrape off all bugs from affected areas and treat them with insecticidal soap. .
Tarnished plant bugs
❮ ❯

These voracious pests will eat almost anything.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Tarnished plant bugs are known to deform leaf tips and stem joins with their excretions.
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    Weeding and application of insecticide every season can prevent infestation.
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Clean off nearby plant debris, then spray with insecticide. There's evidence they don't like sprinkler irrigation.
Imported cabbage worms

These caterpillars will feed on anything from the mustard family.

  • arrow_drop_down_circleSigns & Symptoms
    Look for dispersed holes in leaves.
  • arrow_drop_down_circlePrevention
    The larvae don't like temperatures below 50 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • arrow_drop_down_circleTreatment
    Spray the caterpillars with insecticidal soap.
